In this step, I enable "Windows Security Logging" so Windows records successful and failed logins. This is important because Winlogbeat/ELK needs these logs to show events like "4624" (Success) and "4625" (Failure).

## Steps

1. Open Local Security Policy

   - On my Windows machine, I open "Local Security Policy".
   - I expand "Security Settings".
   - Then I click "Advanced Audit Policy Configuration".

![Step 1 - Open Local Security Policy](images/step1-local-security-policy.png)

2. Go to Audit Logon settings

   - I expand "System Audit Policies - Local Group Policy".
   - I then expand "Logon/Logoff".
   - Then I click "Audit Logon".

![Step 2 - Audit Logon Settings](images/step2-audit-logon.png)

3. Enable Success and Failure Logging

   - I right-click "Audit Logon" and check "Success" and "Failure".
   - Then I click "Apply".

![Step 3 - Enable Success and Failure](images/step3-enable-success-failure.png)

4. Save the settings

   - After clicking Apply, I click "OK" to save and close the window.

![Step 4 - Save Settings](images/step4-save-settings.png)
